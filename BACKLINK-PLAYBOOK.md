## The Quick Truth About Backlinks

- **More links from quality sites = Google trusts you more = better rankings**
- **Domain Authority (DA) matters** — a link from a DA 90 site (Forbes) is worth 100x a link from a DA 20 blog
- **`dofollow` vs `nofollow`** — only dofollow links pass SEO authority. Crunchbase free profiles are nofollow. G2/Capterra reviews are dofollow.
- **Volume matters less than quality** — 5 great backlinks beat 50 bad ones
- **It takes 2-8 weeks** for new backlinks to show up in tracking tools

## Tier 10 — Avoid These Traps

### Don't Buy Backlinks
- Fiverr/Upwork "10 backlinks for $50" services → Google penalty
- Private Blog Networks (PBNs) → Google penalty
- Mass directory submissions to spam directories → ignored or penalized

### Don't Do Bulk Guest Posting
- Random guest posts on unrelated blogs are spam-flagged
- Quality over quantity always wins

### Don't Link-Exchange
- "I'll link to you, you link to me" → Google detects this pattern
- Reciprocal links are de-valued or penalized

### Don't Comment-Spam
- Generic comments with your URL on unrelated posts → ignored at best, penalty at worst
- Substantive comments without dropping URLs build authority

## Backlink Quality Heuristics

Before submitting to a directory or pitching a publication, ask:

1. **Is the site indexed in Google?** Search `site:[domain]` — if no results, skip it
2. **Does it have real traffic?** Check SimilarWeb or Ahrefs free tools
3. **Is the link dofollow?** Right-click → View Source → search for `rel="nofollow"`
4. **Is the audience relevant?** Logistics/freight/supply chain = high value. Generic SaaS directories = medium value. Random directories = low value.
5. **Will Google trust it?** DA 50+ is solid. DA 80+ is excellent.
