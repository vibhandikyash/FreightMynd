## 4. Glossary Expansion — 25 New Terms

Add the following 25 terms to the existing glossary. Each term should have a 2-3 sentence definition optimized for AI citation (start with a direct definitional statement).

1. **Customs Declaration Automation** — The use of AI to extract data from trade documents and pre-populate customs declaration forms, reducing manual data entry by 60-80%.
2. **HS Code Classification** — Harmonized System codes that classify traded goods. AI can automate HS code assignment by analyzing product descriptions, reducing classification errors.
3. **Freight Rate Benchmarking** — The process of comparing carrier rates across lanes, modes, and time periods to identify pricing anomalies and margin optimization opportunities.
4. **Spot Quote** — A one-time freight rate offered for a specific shipment, as opposed to contract rates. Spot quotes require fast turnaround and are prime candidates for AI automation.
5. **RFQ (Request for Quote)** — A buyer's request to carriers or forwarders for pricing on a specific shipment. AI can automate RFQ parsing, rate lookup, and response generation.
6. **eHub (CargoWise)** — CargoWise's integration platform that enables external systems to push and pull data via XML messages. Used by FreightMynd for native TMS integration.
7. **Universal Gateway (CargoWise)** — CargoWise's API layer for real-time data exchange with external applications, supporting event triggers and data synchronization.
8. **BAPI (SAP)** — Business Application Programming Interface — SAP's standard API for integrating external systems with SAP modules including SAP TM (Transportation Management).
9. **Dataverse (Microsoft)** — Microsoft's data platform underlying Dynamics 365, used for storing and managing freight operations data with Power Platform integration.
10. **GLN (Global Location Number)** — A unique 13-digit number used to identify physical locations and legal entities in the supply chain, critical for EDI and customs data exchange.
11. **Document Intelligence** — AI-powered extraction of structured data from unstructured documents (PDFs, images, emails). In freight, this means extracting shipment data from invoices, AWBs, and packing lists.
12. **Confidence Scoring** — A measure (0-100%) of how certain an AI extraction model is about a specific field value. Low-confidence fields are flagged for human review.
13. **Exception Routing** — The process of directing AI-flagged items (low confidence, validation failures, missing data) to human operators for manual review and correction.
14. **Freight Audit** — The process of verifying carrier invoices against contracted rates, shipment records, and service agreements. AI can automate 80-90% of freight audit checks.
15. **Demurrage** — Charges incurred when cargo containers are not picked up or returned within the free time period at a port or terminal. AI can predict and prevent demurrage through proactive alerting.
16. **Detention** — Charges for keeping a shipping container beyond the allowed free time outside the port/terminal. Often confused with demurrage, which applies at the port.
17. **Bill of Lading (BOL)** — A legal document between shipper and carrier detailing the type, quantity, and destination of goods. AI can extract BOL data fields with 95%+ accuracy.
18. **Airway Bill (AWB)** — The freight document for air cargo, serving as a contract of carriage, receipt of goods, and customs declaration support document.
19. **Packing List** — A document detailing the contents, weight, and dimensions of each package in a shipment. Used by customs, carriers, and consignees for verification.
20. **Commercial Invoice** — A customs document declaring the value, quantity, and description of goods being shipped internationally. Primary source for customs duty calculation.
21. **Certificate of Origin** — A document certifying the country of manufacture of goods, required for preferential tariff treatment under trade agreements.
22. **Freight Visibility Platform** — A system that provides real-time tracking and status updates for shipments across carriers, modes, and geographies.
23. **TMS (Transportation Management System)** — Software for planning, executing, and optimizing freight movements. Major TMS platforms include CargoWise, SAP TM, Oracle TMS, and Descartes.
24. **Control Tower (Logistics)** — A centralized operations hub that provides end-to-end visibility and coordination across multiple logistics providers, typically in a 4PL model.
25. **LangGraph** — A framework for building stateful, multi-step AI agent workflows. Used by FreightMynd for orchestrating document extraction, validation, and integration pipelines.
